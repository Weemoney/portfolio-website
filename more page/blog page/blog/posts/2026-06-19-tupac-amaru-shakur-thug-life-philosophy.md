# This is what I want to tell.

I have been living and growing not in my parents' hands, but in many people's hands. You know, I've been on the streets since I was born until when I was 8 years old then I got into my parents' house.

And yet? I still didn't find my parents in the house. I was all alone with my separated big brother. Every day, me and my big bro got out of the house, going to PS2 rental houses or internet cafes, and playing games.

Even in our school, our environment wasn't an urban or "normal" society. There were so many bad people, prostitutes, kids trying to look tough, so many punks, troublemakers. Even the police gave the whole damn public schools in the town a lecture to warn us not to get involved in school gang wars or wild street races, because every week kids dying from that stupid culture.

Every night, in this little town, we saw kids gathering just to talk and play with others, because their parents never cared about them. I was there too. But because of racism on the streets, you know I'm Chinese, and I got discriminated against.

I don't know what happened to me, but every day at school I got bullied, on the streets I got threatened, and at home everything seemed bland. So that's the time when my parents actually tried to be parents.

You know, they're not my real parents, it's my stepmother, and my father was a jerk. He was completely foolish by getting involved with 4 women, and now he's always complaining about his life choices to me.

I mean, everyone kept saying, _"My parents got divorced."_ I was like, _"My parents were never married."_

What I take from my life is, the difficulty of life is not about relationships because there's nothing real and nothing to lose in my social life. So it's just about money, money, and money, you know what I'm saying?

Because I don't see social connections as something important, special or precious. When other people put weight on it, I'm just like, _"I don't care about anybody. You can't stop me with that body."_

And while everyone on the street still cares about their people, I don't even care about my family.
