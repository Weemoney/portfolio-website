# Today I was thinking about my path again.

Sometimes I feel like my life is a game, but not the kind of game where everything is easy and the mission marker is always clear. More like an open world game where I have to figure out the map by myself.

I realized that I have wasted some time, especially with my study. There are things that I should have handled better. Some credits are gone because of my mistakes, and now I have to deal with the consequences. But I don't want to see it only as a failure.

In every game, when you fail a mission, you don't delete the whole save file. You learn the pattern, you understand why you lost, then you prepare better for the next attempt.

My current main quest is clear: win a national marketing competition and build a skill that can make me independent. I don't just want a title or a certificate. I want the ability to create value, sell ideas, understand people, and survive by my own skills.

I think that's why I like marketing. It's not only about selling products. It's about understanding humans. Every person has their own "system", their own motivation, their own weakness. Maybe life is like that too.

Everyone is playing with different stats, different starting points, different maps.
I just need to maximize mine.

Sometimes I still compare myself with other people. I see people who seem to move faster, who already know what they want. But maybe comparing saves from different players doesn't make sense. The only thing that matters is whether my character is stronger than yesterday.

I still have a lot to fix.

My discipline. My consistency. My confidence.

But I also know something: I have survived every version of myself until now.
So maybe the next version will be better.
