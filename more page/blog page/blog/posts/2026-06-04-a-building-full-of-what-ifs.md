# The Life I Imagine Beyond Those Walls

Today, I went on a date with my girlfriend. What made it special was that, for the first time ever, I was brave enough to try sambal. I've never try it because of its smell, so I always avoided it.

The experience made me realize something. I don't trust everybody in this world, but in the end, I trust the world itself. Sometimes, stepping outside my comfort zone leads to unexpected discoveries, and today was one of those moments.

We walked across the university. As we wandered through the campus, I couldn't stop thinking about my own journey.

Sometimes, I feel like I have never truly taken a step toward what I wanted most in life. Lately, I just want to explore the world with the person I love and understand where my path is leading me.

My major, Bioinformatics, belongs to the STEM faculty. Seeing the building again—the architecture, the atmosphere, and the memories attached to it—filled me with quiet sadness. Then we passed by the Computer Science faculty.

The difference hit me immediately. The tall ceilings, the modern architecture, and the vibrant environment were everything I had imagined when I first dreamed of studying Computer Science. It may not be perfect, but to me, it felt far more inspiring than my own faculty.

I became jealous. Years ago, I tried my best to get into Computer Science, but my father did not want me to pursue it. Standing there today, I felt the same regret returning once again. No matter how much time passes, part of me still wonders what my life would have looked like if I had been allowed to choose my own path.

Sometimes, I feel as though I am drowning in that regret. Yet somehow, I keep moving forward.
