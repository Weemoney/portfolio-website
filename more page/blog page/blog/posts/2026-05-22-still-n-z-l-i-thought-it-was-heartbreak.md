My relationships with people have always had a tendency to fall apart. My first friend in kindergarten was an autistic kid, and I loved him genuinely. In elementary school, I had just one friend — someone I was kind to, and loved in the same way. Middle school is where things started getting complex. The bullies were still there, obsessing over me, but I found four friends who were normal people. In the end, I ruined it. High school was no different — one friend, then I ruined it again. Because I'm too direct. I cannot live in emotional uncertainty situation, and I cannot speak in hypocrisy language.

I had a plan to make a change in university. Things weren't going well, and then I met a girl who seemed very different from anyone I'd known before — at least from where I stood. I was just trying to be kind to her. I kept making mistakes in front of her, embarrassed myself, yet she still praised me.

At first I thought, _"Is this hypocrisy? I'm not that foolish. Real Eyez, Realize, Real Lies."_

I already knew the answer. But she was consistent — consistently praising me, consistently building my trust — and slowly, I loosened my guard. That's where the crash began.

I wanted to trust her, even knowing she might be lying, or simply testing me. I wanted what she showed me to be real. She softened my heart. Then the uncertainty crept in, and I felt I had to know the truth — the real answer, directly from her mouth. I already knew it, but I needed to hear it spoken.

And as always, I was right. **Real Eyez, Realize, Real Lies** — even though I never looked deep into her eyes.

I didn't confess anything. I was just being honest.

And then, when I said

_"I'm jealous,"_

I meant it as a compliment

_"you're a force of nature, so anyone who has you is lucky, and I'm jealous, because for a brief moment, you made my dream feel real."_

But the friendship was ruined.

I don't mind.

As long as I'm free from the chains that were pulling me under.

At this point, this isn't about heartbreak. It's about truth, or I might called it **The Rhapsody Of Uncertainty**
