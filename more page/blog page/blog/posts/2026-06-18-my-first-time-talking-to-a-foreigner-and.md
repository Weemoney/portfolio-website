# First step to home

I always walk through the covered walkway in front of the campus library whenever I'm going home, you know? That block covers so many crucial buildings. The walkway connects the library, international office, bank, career center, canteen, and when I got to my *237th* step on that walkway, I saw someone pacing around the park. The guy had yellow hair, was wearing glasses, and then he sat alone on a bench along the walkway, opening his Bible. I don't know where I got my guts, but I just wanted to talk to him.

So I said, _"Hi! Excuse me, sir! May I know you?"_ and he was like, *wait, what? Then slowly, he directed his hand to the bench in front of him, inviting me to sit.

I just wanted to know:
_"What brings you here? Are you a professor? A student? Which country do you come from?"_

He explained his reason for being on this campus. He's from Arizona. I joked with him about cowboys at first, saying,_"You're a cowboy, right? You do rodeo at homeland, eh? Yeehaa!"_ He burst out laughing and reluctantly said yes while smiling.

He's an agriculture bachelor's alumnus from Arizona State University, and he's doing his first semester at Brawijaya University for a master's degree in agricultural technology.

We shared a lot of stories about the differences in academic culture between Indonesia and US colleges. But the part I want to tell is, I asked him:

_"Why did you choose Indonesia? You have great facilities and an environment in the US, don't you?"_

He started opening his Bible. He recited Proverbs 3:27 to me, and my inner monologue was like _"He's a missionary."_

That was a great experience for me. He ate rawon and said he liked it.
